# Intro

## Core Insight
> Clarity beats cleverness: position your customer as the hero and your brand as the guide using a seven-part story framework to cut through noise and drive action.

---

## Representative Samples: 4

### Sample 1: The Customer Is the Hero, Not You
**Scenario**: A software company leads its homepage with "We've won 12 awards!" but visitors bounce because they can't instantly see how the product solves their problem.
**Pattern**: Shift messaging from brand-centric to customer-centric. When people see themselves as the protagonist on a journey, engagement rises because the story feels personally relevant.

### Sample 2: Solve Three Layers of Problem
**Scenario**: A fitness app markets "Track your workouts" (external), but ignores the user's internal shame ("I'm not disciplined") and philosophical frustration ("Health shouldn't be this confusing").
**Pattern**: Address external (practical), internal (emotional), and philosophical (moral) problems. Messaging that resonates on all three layers builds deeper trust and motivation to act.

### Sample 3: Be the Guide, Not the Guru
**Scenario**: A financial advisor opens with "I'm a CFP with 20 years experience" but prospects still hesitate to book a call.
**Pattern**: Lead with empathy ("I know money stress keeps you up") before competence ("Here's my plan"). Trust forms when people feel understood first, then guided.

### Sample 4: Make the Next Step Obvious
**Scenario**: An e-commerce site says "Learn more about our sustainable materials" but visitors leave because the call-to-action is passive and vague.
**Pattern**: Use direct, urgent calls to action ("Get your free sample kit today") paired with a simple 3-step plan. Reduce friction by telling people exactly what to do next and why it matters.

## Key Generalizations

| Concept | Core Truth |
|---------|-----------|
| **Clarity > Creativity** | Confused minds don't buy; simple, story-structured messages convert |
| **Story is a survival tool** | Humans are wired to follow narratives that promise resolution and transformation |
| **Guide credibility = empathy + proof** | Authority alone repels; empathy first, then evidence, builds trust |

## Conclusion
Start with one SB7 element this week: rewrite your homepage headline to put the customer's desire first, then test the change. Remember: **Message Clarity = (Hero Desire × Guide Empathy) ÷ Noise**. You don't need to overhaul everything at once—clarifying one sentence using the story framework creates momentum for the rest.
